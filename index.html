About Me

I began my computer science journey in high school when I took Computer Programming I and II. The class was fun and seemed more interesting than any other course I've taken so I decided to commit to a Computer Science degree. That was back in 2016 and now as senior at The University of Texas at San Antonio, I am excited to start my journey not as a student but as a computer scientist!

I love participating in the student led organizations on campus! I am heavily invovled in ACM UTSA as I have served as President for the Association of Computing Machinery - Women's chapter. One of my goals as a female student in CS is to help the underrepresented and underprivileged students in the computer science field to thrive. I love spending time to promote inclusivity and diversity! This has led me to join ACM-W and start UTSA's first ever marginalized gender centric hackathon.

I believe that work life balance is the key to success - which leads me to balance my life through my interests in hiking, gardening, photography, painting, and much more! I am currently a Software Engineer Intern for JP Morgan & Chase Summer 2021.
